Poverty Traps (May 2022 - Present (as Honors Thesis))

Advisor: Prof. Brandon Lehr, Economics Professor at Occidental College

SEE CV FOR PAPER. Poverty traps are self-reinforcing mechanisms where individuals, despite maximum effort, can never leave poverty. Mathematical models help us determine how they arise and how policy might effectively stop them. My research covers a large literature review of poverty traps models, a rederivation of the model explored in Banerjee and Newman, 1994, and extending their work with behavioral economic theory. Specifically, I incorporate the theory of loss aversion o capture unique poverty trap dynamics.
